# Logistics Inventory Optimization & Supply Chain Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes a logistics and warehouse inventory dataset using SQL to uncover insights into inventory performance, demand patterns, stockout risks, warehouse efficiency, and inventory turnover.

The objective of the analysis is to help supply chain managers make data-driven decisions that improve inventory availability, reduce stockouts, optimize warehouse operations, and enhance overall supply chain performance.

Through a combination of exploratory analysis, business-focused KPIs, and advanced SQL techniques, this project identifies operational bottlenecks and opportunities for inventory optimization.

---

## Business Problem

Logistics and supply chain organizations face several challenges:

* Maintaining optimal inventory levels
* Preventing stockouts and lost sales
* Improving warehouse efficiency
* Managing products with unpredictable demand
* Ensuring reorder points align with future demand forecasts

This analysis aims to answer key business questions and provide actionable recommendations to improve inventory management and operational performance.

## Key Business Questions

### Inventory Management

* Which products are below their reorder point?
* Which categories hold the highest inventory value?
* Which products are at risk of future stock shortages?

### Demand Analysis

* Which products have the highest demand?
* Which categories experience the greatest demand?
* Which products show the highest demand variability?

### Stockout Analysis

* Which categories experience the most stockouts?
* How do stockouts impact order fulfillment performance?

### Warehouse Performance

* Which warehouse zones have the longest picking times?
* Which zones demonstrate the highest operational efficiency?

### Inventory Turnover

* Which products move through inventory the fastest?
* Which categories achieve the highest turnover rates?

---

## Analysis Performed

### Inventory Health Analysis

Evaluated current stock levels against reorder points to identify products requiring replenishment.

### Demand Analysis

Analyzed daily demand patterns and demand variability to identify high-demand and high-risk inventory items.

### Stockout Analysis

Investigated stockout frequency and its relationship with customer order fulfillment rates.

### Warehouse Efficiency Analysis

Measured operational efficiency using warehouse picking times and layout efficiency scores.

### Inventory Valuation

Calculated inventory value using stock levels and unit prices to identify high-value inventory categories.

### Turnover Analysis

Assessed inventory turnover ratios to determine which products and categories move most efficiently through the supply chain.

### Forecast-Based Risk Analysis

Compared current stock levels against forecasted demand to identify future inventory shortages.

Business Recommendations

1. Increase safety stock levels for products with high demand variability.
2. Review reorder point thresholds for products frequently experiencing stockouts.
3. Optimize warehouse layouts in low-efficiency zones.
4. Prioritize monitoring of high-value inventory categories.
5. Implement demand-driven replenishment strategies using forecasted demand data.
6. Develop early warning systems for products projected to fall below future inventory requirements.
